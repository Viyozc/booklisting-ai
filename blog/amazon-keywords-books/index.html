Amazon Keywords for Books: The 9 Places They Actually Matter

Updated 2026-07-22 · BookListing team

Amazon reads keywords in more places than you think

Authors fixate on the 7 keyword fields and ignore the rest. Amazon's A9 also weights your title, subtitle, series name, author central categories, and description. A free KDP listing audit scores coverage across all of them so you see the gaps.

The 7 backend keyword fields

Each field accepts a short phrase up to 50 characters. "small town romance" and "enemies to lovers" are distinct fields, not one. Fill all seven with multi-word phrases; don't waste a field on a single generic word Amazon already sees elsewhere.

Title and subtitle

Your title is the strongest keyword signal. The subtitle is where you sneak in a second phrase without spamming the title — "A Small-Town Enemies-to-Lovers Romantic Comedy" covers two queries at once. Keep it readable; Amazon penalizes keyword soup.

Categories (Author Central)

Beyond the 2 KDP categories, you can request more via Author Central. Each category is a keyword cluster readers browse. More relevant categories = more shelf presence = more discovery.

Description

Amazon indexes the description. Naturally include your top 2–3 phrases in the first 150 characters and again lower down. Use bold and line breaks to stay scannable — readable descriptions convert better, which lifts ranking.
